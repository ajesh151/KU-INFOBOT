kucat|Kathmandu University Common Admission Test (KUCAT) is a Computer-Based Test conducted for engineering admissions.

entrance exam|Admission to the School of Engineering requires qualifying the Kathmandu University Common Admission Test (KUCAT-CBT).

computer based test|The entrance examination is conducted in Computer-Based Test (CBT) mode.

test format|The examination consists of 120 multiple-choice questions.

duration|The examination duration is 120 minutes (2 hours).

subjects|The entrance examination contains 40 questions each from Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics.

syllabus|Questions are prepared according to the official Kathmandu University entrance syllabus.

adaptive scoring|The examination uses adaptive scoring where the difficulty of the next question depends on the previous answer.

difficulty level|Questions are divided into five difficulty levels.

level 1|Level 1 questions carry 11 points.

level 2|Level 2 questions carry 13 points.

level 3|Level 3 questions carry 15 points.

level 4|Level 4 questions carry 17 points.

level 5|Level 5 questions carry 19 points.

negative marking|There is no traditional negative marking. Incorrect answers lower the difficulty level of subsequent questions.

score range|The total obtainable score ranges from 0 to 2220.

pass mark|The minimum qualifying score is 528.

minimum score|The minimum score required to qualify the entrance examination is 528.

qualifying score|Applicants must obtain at least 528 marks to qualify.

computer engineering cutoff|The estimated merit score for Computer Engineering ranges from 900 to 1300 or above.

computer engineering merit|The estimated admission score for Computer Engineering ranges from 900 to 1300 or above.

ai cutoff|The estimated merit score for B.Tech Artificial Intelligence ranges from 800 to 1100 or above.

civil engineering cutoff|The estimated merit score for Civil Engineering (Hydropower) ranges from 700 to 1100 or above.

mechanical engineering cutoff|The estimated merit score for Mechanical Engineering ranges from 600 to 1000 or above.

mining engineering cutoff|The estimated merit score for Mining Engineering ranges from 528 to 700 or above.
